CSU Under Grad Research

During my senior year at CSUSM I completed one year of under graduate research (CHEM 499, 399) in bioinformatics. Working under Sajith Jayasinghe Ph.D. we developed two command line tools in Python, for parsing FASTA files of bacterial transmembrane proteins, in order to compare differences in how inner and outer membrane segments get introduced into the membrane by the translocon. Currently the rational is that hydrophobicity is the determining factor, but we have found evidence that proves otherwise. [link to reseach paper website, once published]

About me

I am 25, I have a B.S. in Biochemistry from CSU San Marcos and I am eager to begin my career in the software industry. After five years of preparing for medical school, I realized during a drug discovery internship at a pharmaceutical company, that the medical path was not for me. Around the same time I began taking online courses in computer programming and I fell in love instantly. I realized that everything I enjoyed about mathematics, chemistry, and being a scientist, was offered in software engineering with the enormous benefit of being incredibly accessible. My passion for life science still remains, although now I desire to solve problems in this field through the utilization of detailed software designed specifically for the task at hand. Suprisingly the amount of newly emerging tools and software available to scientists each year remains untouched due to lack of understanding or ease of implementation. I hope to change this by delivering fast and robust solutions to life scientists, that can be implemented without understanding programming.
